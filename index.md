You have put time and effort into bulding all of your courses in Moodle LMS when your school has decided to migrate into Canvas LMS. Now you have to start all over, right?

This guide was created so to ensure that you minimize the amount of work you need to do in order to transition your course from Moodle into Canvas. This way, you will not have to start over from scratch.

---

## Before You Export {#before-export}

Before we download all of our Moodle content and upload it into Canvas, we want to make sure everything is as Canvas-ready as possible. Because Moodle and Canvas are not isomorphic content-wise, we need to make everything as Canvas-friendly first, before downloading it from Moodle. Additionally, since you presumable have experience working in Moodle, it will be easier to transition everything while it is still in Moodle. This way, the learning curve for Canvas is kept to a minimum.

So how do we modify our Moodle content so that it is Canvas-ready? We first need to understand the different types of content Moodle creates and then find its counterpart "buddy" in Canvas. This way, we can predict the structure and behavior of our content in Canvas before we even begin exploring our Canvas course.
